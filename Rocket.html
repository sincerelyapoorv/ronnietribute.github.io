Personal Life

How old is Ronnie O'Sullivan?

Ronnie O’Sullivan was born on the 5th of December 1975. He is 45-years-old and will soon turn 46.

How tall is Ronnie O'Sullivan?

According to Eurosport, Ronnie O’Sullivan is 1.80 meters (5’9) tall.

Where does Ronnie O'Sullivan come from?

Unlike popular belief, Ronnie O'Sullivan is not Irish but has Irish ancestry. The professional English snooker player was born in Wordsley, West Midlands, England. O’Sullivan was brought up in Chigwell, Essex, where he currently resides.

Is Ronnie O'Sullivan married?

Ronnie O’Sullivan is not married currently. He has been with actress Laila Rouass for nine years and they have been engaged since 2013.

Does Ronnie O'Sullivan have a son?

O’Sullivan has three children: Taylor-Ann Magnus, Lily and Ronnie Jr.

Snooker Life

When did Ronnie O’Sullivan start playing snooker?

The snooker prodigy began playing snooker at 7 years old. He won his first club tournament at 9-years-old, and one year later, at 10 years of age, he made his first competitive century break.

What was the first championship won by Ronnie O’Sullivan?

At 13-years-old, Ronnie O’Sullivan won the British Under-16 Championship.

When did Ronnie O’Sullivan start playing as a professional?

Ronnie O’Sullivan turned professional in 1992 at age 16.

How many competitive maximum breaks does Ronnie O’Sullivan have?

Ronnie O’Sullivan achieved his first professional maximum break at 15-years-old. Since then, he achieved fourteen more. With 15 competitive maximum breaks, Ronnie O’Sullivan holds the record for the most maximum breaks in a competitive setting.

What was Ronnie O’Sullivan’s fastest competitive maximum break?

Ronnie’s fastest competitive maximum break took him 5 minutes and 8 seconds. The English player holds the record for the fastest professional maximum break ever. He achieved this break at the 1997 World Championship.

How many centuries does Ronnie O’Sullivan have?

Ronnie O’Sullivan has over 1,000 century breaks professionally. He achieved his 1,000 century during the 2019 Players Championship, and he’s currently the only professional player holding more than 1,000 centuries.

How many titles does Ronnie O’Sullivan hold?

Ronnie O’Sullivan has won a record 20 Triple Crown titles during his professional career. He also has won 37 ranking titles, a record for the most ranking titles. The English player also holds 31 professional non-ranking event titles and 3 minor-ranking event titles.

Ronnie is a six-time world champion. He won the World Championship in 2001, 2004, 2008, 2012, 2013, and most recently in 2020. The Rocket has also won the Masters seven times, in 1995, 2005, 2007, 2009, 2014, 2016 and 2017. Also, he holds a record seven-time champion of the UK Championship, having won in 1993, 1997, 2001, 2007, 2014, 2017, and 2018.

What's Ronnie O'Sullivan's net worth?

It is believed that Ronnie O’Sullivan has built a net worth of around £10 million.

What is Ronnie O’Sullivan’s current world ranking?

The Rocket currently holds third place in the snooker world rankings.

Ronnie O’Sullivan wins record-equalling seventh world title

Ronnie O’Sullivan claims his seventh world title after beating Judd Trump 18-13 at the World Championship final on Monday. The Rocket has equalled the record set by Stephen Hendry, who reached seven world titles in 1999.

With a lead of 12-5 after the Sunday sessions, it seemed that O’Sullivan would clinch the title as soon as he arrived at the Crucible Theatre in Sheffield. However, it was not that easy.

Trump fought back and managed to shorten O’Sullivan’s lead, finishing the afternoon session with 14-11. O’Sullivan had a lead of three frames before the evening session.

The Rocket came back to the last session at full force. With breaks of 82, 85, 75 and 85, O’Sullivan ends triumphant and wins another Crucible title. At 46-years-old, Ronnie O'Sullivan also became the oldest world champion in the Crucible history.

“I tried to be as relaxed as I could, but that is probably the greatest result I’ve had against somebody like Judd [Trump]. Said O’Sullivan.

“I’ve never bothered about records. When you get them, it’s kind of nice. I don’t have targets. I’ve loved every tournament this year, I’ve just loved playing.”

After winning, O’Sullivan suggested he would be back in Sheffield next year to fully break the record, joking that he would “share it [the record] for a year” with Hendry.

Even though Trump ended up on the losing side, he could not hide his admiration for O’Sullivan sharing how delightful it was to share a table with The Rocket.

"I was just glad to make a match out of it," Trump said to the BBC. "It's been a pleasure to share a table with him.

"It's an amazing achievement and he's the best player of all time - he keeps getting better and better. His determination and dedication are clear to see. He's been the best player in this tournament by quite a distance."

O’Sullivan won his first world title in 2001, and since then he has been a force to be reckoned with. Even though he considered retirement in 2011, he has turned his form around and today, the Englishman has 39 ranking titles under his belt plus, a series of other astonishing achievements, making him truly one of the greatest of all time.

Just at the Crucible, he’s broken a few records. O’Sullivan has won 74 matches, made 199 centuries, won 1,182 frames and won the most amount of prize money - £3,061,700. These are some of his Crucible records but there are also a few left to break, including most maximums where he sits at 3 equalling with Stephen Hendry and most titles.
